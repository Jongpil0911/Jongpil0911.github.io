# Frame-Stacking Method for Dark Digital Holographic Microscopy to Acquire 3D Profiles in a Low-Power Laser Environment
## Frame-Stacking Method (FSM) for Dark DHM
### Frame-Stacking Method
This figure shows the difference between 3D profiles and cross-sectional height profiles along the x-axis in a normal environment before and after passing through the ND filter.
![Comparison of the Hologram and Dark Hologram](/assets/img/FSM/compare.png)
Figure (a) shows 3D profiles in the normal environment, and Figure (b) shows 3D profiles in a low-power laser environment.
The figure(b) seems to be noisy, and the shape of the object cannot be recognized.
With respect to the cross-sectional height profile along the x-axis, the elliptical shape characteristic of microspheres can be observed in Figure (a), but not in Figure (b).
In other words, the accuracy of 3D profiles is low.
Since it is difficult to recognize and diagnose the object in Figure (b), we aim to achieve an accuracy to the extent that the shape of the object can be observed by using the Frame-Stacking Method (FSM) for dark DHM.
The flowcharts of the conventional 3D process in DHM and the FSM are presented in the next figure.
![Compare the flowchart of the Hologram image processing and Dark Hologram image processing](/assets/img/FSM/flowchart.png)
In the case of recording objects, compared with the conventional methods, the proposed method captures videos instead of images.
The video is frame-decomposed, and the image processing of superimposition and rescaling is performed.
On the other hand, for recording reference images, the glass slide is completely removed from the object arm of the interferometer and captured as a single image, as in the conventional method.
In addition, the power at that time is the normal power without an ND filter.
This is because there is no object in the reference image.
After this processing, the 3D image is converted from a reference image and an object image, achieving a highly accurate 3D image even in low-power environments.

### Advance of FSM
Sufficient information can be obtained by superimposing the frame-resolved hologram images.
The equation shows an improved hologram image for FSM.
$$I_{Ho-FSM}(x, y) = \frac{1}{\alpha} \sum^{N}_{i = 1}I_i^{HoD}(x, y)$$
$$\alpha = \frac{max\left\{ \sum^{N}_{i = 1} I_i^{HoD} (x, y) \right\}}{255}$$
where the $I_{Ho-FSM}(x, y)$ is obtained when the image $I_i^{HoD}$ taken in a low-power laser environment is superimposed and rescaled.
$\alpha$ is the superimposed **luminance value rescaled to 8 bits**.
$N$ is the total number of frames, $i$ is the frame number, and $x, y$ are the pixel indices of the columns and rows.
In addition, random noise such as camera noise and detection noise is added to the image.
Under the normal-power laser environment, the effect of the noise on the 3D profile is small because the object information is much more than the noise, but the effect of the noise is huge under the low-power laser environment.
However, the object information can be corrected by superimposing it in the FSM.
In addition, since the object information has a huge amount of information compared with the noise, the noise can be compressed to reduce the effect of the noise to a level which is no effect on accuracy.

### Quantitative Comparison
#### Microsphere
|  Method  |          MSE           | PSNR [dB] |  SSIM   |
| :------: | :--------------------: | :-------: | :-----: |
| Original | $2.35 \times 10^{-11}$ |   $106$   | $0.384$ |
|   FSM    | $3.27 \times 10^{-12}$ |   $115$   | $0.507$ |

#### Red Blood Cels
 Since DHM is intended to be applied to cells such as the red blood cells (RBCs) and microorganisms, we conducted experiments using RBCs as objects.
 First, the hologram image of the RBCs taken without the ND filter, and the 3D profile and cross-sectional height profile are shown in following Figure.
![Original data of the RBCs](/assets/img/FSM/rbcs.png)
 This is the result of the windowing and profiling of RBCs in the red box in Figure.
 The central indentation characteristic of RBCs can be seen with respect to the cross-sectional height profile.
 Figure shows the comparison results of the 3D profiles and cross-sectional height profile obtained by the conventional method and FSM superimposed on 100 frames of RBCs at a power of 0.015mW.
 ![Compare of the Hologram and Dark Hologram](/assets/img/FSM/rbc_compare.png)
Compared to Figure (a), the noise was reduced in Fig.(b), and a profile of RBCs similar to the result under the normal power environment, as shown Fig., was obtained.
In the cross-sectional height profile, the noise in the center of the profile can be seen in Fig.(a) compared to Fig.(b).
In addition, the SSIM values in Figs.(a), (b) are 0.186 and 0.203, respectively.
It is noticed that the SSIM by the proposed method is 1.09 times more accurate than the SSIM by the conventional method.
Therefore, FSM is also effective for RBCs.

| :------: | :---: |
| Original | 0.186 |
|   FSM    | 0.203 |
